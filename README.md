# Module 3 — Safe Browsing Challenge

Browser Security & URL Analysis Challenge
The padlock is lying to you.
That green lock icon in your browser's address bar does not mean a website is safe. It never did. It means the connection between your device and the server is encrypted — which is useful, but completely irrelevant when the server belongs to an attacker. Millions of phishing sites operate behind valid HTTPS certificates, and millions of people hand over their credentials every year because they saw a padlock and felt reassured.
Safe Browsing Challenge dismantles that misconception — and a dozen others — through twelve fully rendered browser simulations. Each scenario presents a realistic website complete with a working address bar, SSL indicator, navigation chrome, and page content. Your task is to judge whether the site is genuine or a carefully constructed fake.
The scenarios cover the full spectrum of URL-based deception: plaintext HTTP on a fake bank login, obvious prize scam domains, the HTTPS misconception with a convincing PayPal clone, subdomain tricks that hide a fake domain behind a legitimate-looking brand name, a Philippine government impersonation exploiting the fact that most people don't know official sites always use .gov.ph, a Facebook clone registered at a domain that looks right until you read it closely, a Microsoft OAuth relay with a path structure copied from the real login flow, and a Google typosquat where two letters have been silently replaced with zeros.
After each answer, the URL is annotated in place — the real domain highlighted in red, the spoofed brand name shown for exactly what it is.

## What you'll walk away with

The ability to read a URL properly, the permanent correction of the HTTPS misconception, and the knowledge that visual design tells you absolutely nothing about whether a website is safe.

## CyberSafe for Girls in ICT Day

CyberSafe was developed to support the ITU's Girls in ICT Day mission of empowering women and girls to pursue careers in technology — and to ensure that every woman who enters the digital world does so equipped to protect herself in it.
